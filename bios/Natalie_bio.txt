Me 
Hello! I'm Natalie Miller, and I'm passionate about helping students build confidence and 
success in math. 
As a 20-year resident of West Sonoma County, I'm deeply connected to the local community 
and dedicated to supporting students and families in the area. For the past four years, I have 
worked as a substitute teacher throughout Sonoma County, gaining experience with students 
across a variety of grade levels and learning styles. I am currently pursuing my 
Foundational-Level Mathematics Teaching Credential with the goal of teaching middle school 
math full-time. 
I specialize in working with elementary and middle school students, helping them strengthen 
their math skills, overcome challenges, and develop a positive attitude toward learning. I believe 
every student learns differently, and I strive to meet students where they are—building on their 
strengths, addressing their individual needs, and helping them progress at a pace that supports 
both understanding and confidence. 
My favorite part of tutoring is witnessing those exciting “aha!” moments when a concept finally 
clicks and a student realizes they can do something they once thought was difficult. Those 
moments are what inspire me to teach. 
When I'm not teaching or studying, I enjoy mountain biking, spending time with my two dogs, 
and being with my family. I'm happily married and the proud parent of two adult children. 
I believe every student can succeed in math with the right support, encouragement, and 
instruction. My goal is to create a patient, engaging learning environment where students feel 
comfortable asking questions, taking risks, and growing into confident problem-solvers. 
Meeting students where they are, building confidence, and creating those “aha!” 
moments that make math click.
